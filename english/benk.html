TERRARIUM BENCH

This is an upcycling project where I have built a bench from materials saved from the landfill. All the wood is Indian rosewood and is taken from an old table that was about to be thrown away. The top glass plate is an old shelf that was also going to be thrown away but was saved at the last minute. The side glass walls are taken from old mirrors where the mirror film has been removed and the glass has been cut to the correct dimensions.
A closed terrarium is a self-sufficient ecosystem that recycles water and nutrients, creating a humid, stable environment that requires no maintenance. The moss inside, taken from the school's roof, would otherwise have been thrown away during spring cleaning.

Build Process Summary

Materials

This is the table from which the materials were taken. Many give these away on Finn because for them it has become an old and worn piece of furniture, but underneath hides a beautiful material.

Turning the Legs

The legs of the bench are the old table legs, solid wood in Indian rosewood. The legs were a bit too thick and had some damage, so they were turned down to the right size and shape. This also brought out the beautiful color of the wood that was hidden under old varnish.

Ensuring a Transition

The attachment to the glass terrarium is made by creating a groove in each leg that the terrarium can rest in. This also provides a nice transition from wood to glass and hides the corners of the terrarium.

Test Fit

First test assembly to check if the dimensions are correct. Early in the process, it often requires a series of adjustments and solving various challenges.

First Assembly

Here you see the first assembly of almost all the components. The only thing missing is the stainless steel resting points on top of the legs.
